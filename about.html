About Me

Who I Am

I didn’t start out set on Computer Engineering — I was originally drawn to more hands-on fields like architecture and traditional engineering. I chose Computer Engineering for its flexibility, but at first, the concepts felt abstract and disconnected from real-world impact. That changed during a project-based lab where I built my first IoT system — an automated plant watering device using Arduino, a soil moisture sensor, a relay, and the Blynk app. That was the first time I saw hardware and software working together to solve a real problem, and it completely changed how I approached engineering. From there, I became more intentional about learning through projects. I explored Python, SQL, and web development, and later C++, which I particularly enjoy for its structure and low-level control. Over time, I developed a strong interest in embedded systems and AI/ML, especially at the intersection of hardware and intelligent software systems. I’m also drawn to understanding how complex tools and systems are built under the hood. I enjoy debugging and problem-solving — not just getting things to work, but understanding why they fail. I’ve learned that stepping back and iterating is often just as important as writing code. Right now, I’m still exploring my path between embedded systems and intelligent systems, with a focus on building real-world solutions that combine hardware and software in meaningful ways. For me, engineering is about creating systems that don’t just exist in code — but work in the real world.
